Cable and Cable Management

Rexel cable management capabilities include a wide array of options that can be catered to your specific site needs. We stock a broad range of wire and cable for both onshore and offshore exploration, and production environments, and have a cable depot location in Houston, Texas close to our Oil and Gas operations offices.

We are the leading and largest supplier of General Cable IEEE 1580 Type P, E, X cables in the US. In addition, we supply Welded Corrugated Aluminum Molded cables for MC-HL applications, IEC NEK 606 and IEC 92-3 cables, and all types of communication cables that meet global industry standards and demands.

We also specialize in value-added cable services, including:

  • Cable cutting and spooling to your specific project requirements
  • Cable assemblies (built and tested to your specifications), including service loops and cable disconnects
  • Cable optimization procedures to reduce scrap and minimize overruns
  • Tagging and kitting services to satisfy unique job-site logistical needs
  • Flexible pricing programs to help you manage your costs when prices(like copper) fluctuate
  • Design and specification assistance to aid in cable standardization

Product Rationalization

When new technologies are introduced into the market they often replace existing, outdated products that you have in inventory. This leads to clutter, redundancy and waste. We can seamlessly direct you to the latest technologies and help you phase out old product.

Training & Technical Education

Reporting Capabilities

Drill into your purchases by facility, manufacturer or part number with our reports customized to your needs. Reports can be run weekly, monthly, quarterly and annually. Lighting & Energy Audits

We can perform comprehensive lighting audits whether it be refinery, offshore platforms or compressor stations — whatever your facility. We also coordinate all rebates and incentives, and deliver the safest and most energy efficient solutions for your needs.

Lower Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

Reduce operating expenses by understanding your Total Cost of Ownership. We examine your application, the cost associated with backup materials, production efficiencies, downtime and maintenance costs to identify areas for beneficial process modifications.
